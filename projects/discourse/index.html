Discourse - Political Chat Web App

A hackathon project that pairs people of differing viewpoints and uses AI to encourage civil conversation

At the 2019 Irish Hacks Hackathon, I worked in a group of 3 to develop this web app. The concept is to provide a platform that encourages people on different sides of the political spectrum to engage in constructive discourse and bridge their idealogical differences. Users are prompted to fill out a brief questionnaire to identify their lean on various issues, and then a list of users with different opinions are suggested to them. The application looks for similar attributes and suggests them as a starting point, and then steers them toward areas of conversation where they have less agreement. In order to encourage civility, the program uses IBM Watson's Tone Analysis function to screen for inflammatory comments, and recomends that the user revise their message to use more civil language. In addition to the chat website, we developed a browser extension that applies the same message screening to input elements on any website. Therefore, users on other chat sites like social media platforms receive the same warning message for angry comments, and the extension suggests that they head to the Discourse site to continue their discussion.

Quick Information

  • Languages: HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Frameworks/Backends: IBM Watson, Google Firebase, Node Red
  • Won the IBM Watson Award at 2019 Irish Hacks for incorperation of AI
