About

I am a current first year student at the University of Notre Dame planning to major in Computer Science and/or Applied and Computational Statistics and Mathematics (AMCS). I am highly self motivated and curious about problem solving. Besides my core passions, I'm interested in areas ranging from music to politics, and I seeks out problems or ideas that link these topics together.

Over time, I've tinkered with a lot of ideas, and my curiosity sometimes grows into extended experiments. I've independently learned most of my programming knowledge in order to pursue these projects. My primary languages are C/C++ and Python, although I've also branched out into HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, SQL, and x86 Assembly. I've put some of my larger work under the Projects tab where you can also find links to the source code on Github.

EXPERIENCES

  • Computation Fluid Dynamics Research Project

    Over my Junior and Senior years in high school, I worked on an independent science project looking into how to better predict the formation of turbulence with fluids. While fluids behavior is governed by equations discovered over a century ago, I was fascinated by how difficult it still is to understand turbulence. After reading a paper about a laboratory technique that significantly simplified this problem, I looked at whether computer simulations could be used in the same way to speed up this type of research. Eventually, I created a custom fluid simulation in C++ and conducted hundreds of trials. Most of the results were ultimately inconclusive, but the technique showed potential if used with more computational resources. The project was presented at the CT STEM and CT Science and Engineering Fairs and recieved a number of awards and honors.

  • Eagle Project

    I was a Boy Scout for over 6 years and completed my Eagle project and the Eagle rank in 2018. The project involved clearing a large, overgrown garden at a local school and putting in new design that is much easier to maintain. It involved gathering over a thousand dollars in material donations and 140 volunteer hours to complete.

  • Science Bowl Website

    My high school science bowl team wanted a way to track their performance in practices so that they could understand they could identify what topics they needed to focus on. I developed and maintained a basic website for the club for meeting this need. The site provided an easy interface for recording practices and seeing both team and individual analysis. To learn more about the website, check out its projects page.

  • Carleton Summer Computer Science Institute

    In the summer of 2018, I did a 3-week computer science program at Carleton College. During this, I got exposure to digital music creation, robots, and computer vision with OpenCV. As a part of the program, I worked with a partner on a small research project where we ultimately made device that automatically generated songs. The device allowed human interaction for changing the volume, tempo, and style of the music.

  • Music

    I deeply enjoy listening to and making music, and I've played the trumpet for 8 years. During high school, I was part of my school's wind ensemble and jazz band, and I participated in the Connecticut All-State Music Festival four times. Other work I've done includes playing taps for Veteran's Day events and taking part in the pit orchestra for a community theatre production.
